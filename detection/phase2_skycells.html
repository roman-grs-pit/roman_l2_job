Phase 2 — HLWAS expansion per skycell

For each selected skycell, enumerate every HLWAS F158 (pointing, SCA) that overlaps

What Phase 2 does

Phase 2 is purely geometric — no image data is simulated. For each of the 12 skycells selected in Phase 1:

  1. Gather all HLWAS F158 (Wide-Field2) exposures whose nominal centre is within 0.6° of the skycell centre — the pool of candidate pointings whose outer SCAs could plausibly reach the 4.6′ skycell.
  2. For each (candidate pointing, SCA) pair, build the CRDS-backed WCS via romanisim.wcs.get_wcs(meta_dict, usecrds=True) with fill_in_parameters(coord, pa_aper, boresight=False) — the same code path romanisim-make-image --usecrds uses.
  3. Take the 4 SCA corners and call romancal.skycell.match.find_skycell_matches(corners). If the selected skycell is in the match list, add the (pointing, SCA) to Phase 4’s sim list.

All L2s per skycell are simulated at the skycell’s SIM_DATE (from Phase 1, chosen for ~90° solar elongation). That fixes zodi across the mosaic regardless of what HLWAS PASS the original visit was scheduled in.

Per-skycell coverage maps

Design consequence — variable per-pixel depth across the sample

Low-coverage skycells (pairs 1, 2, 5, 6) have only ~2 L2 files each after expansion — outer SCAs of ~1-2 nearby HLWAS visits. Per-pixel depth will be 1-2 in most of those mosaics.

High-coverage skycells (pairs 3, 4, 7, 8, 11, 12) have 16-26 L2 files each, with per-pixel depth ~8-12 expected based on pair 11119’s earlier validation.

That dynamic range — factor of 10× in depth across the sample — is the point of the matrix design: Phase 5 measures detection efficiency vs position, source type, magnitude, and depth simultaneously.

Reproducing

pixi run python scripts/detection/02_skycell_overlap.py

Reads selected_skycells.ecsv (Phase 1), writes pointings_sca_to_simulate.ecsv (the Phase 4 input) plus per-pair coverage plots. Wall time ~85 min at 4-way parallelism (one CRDS-backed get_wcs × find_skycell_matches call takes a few seconds; the expansion loops over # candidates × 18 SCAs per pair).

Checkpoint

Phase 2 done — 10 of 12 skycells have non-zero L2 coverage. The two skipped (pairs 9, 10 in the high_zodi × low_coverage cell) are documented in Phase 1.

## L2 counts per skycell

```{python}
#| echo: false
#| tbl-cap: "L2 files per pair after HLWAS expansion. `n_l2_files` is the distinct (PASS × SEGMENT × VISIT × EXPOSURE × SCA) combos whose SCA overlaps the target skycell; `n_visits` is the number of distinct HLWAS visits contributing. Pairs 9 and 10 got 0 overlap — HLWAS tiling near the ecliptic leaves coverage gaps."
dedup_key = ["PASS", "SEGMENT", "OBSERVATION", "VISIT", "EXPOSURE", "SCA"]
visits_key = ["PASS", "SEGMENT", "OBSERVATION", "VISIT"]

l2_counts = (NEED_SIM.drop_duplicates(["PAIR_ID"] + dedup_key)
             .groupby("PAIR_ID").size().reset_index(name="n_l2_files"))
l2_counts["n_visits"] = (NEED_SIM.drop_duplicates(["PAIR_ID"] + visits_key)
                         .groupby("PAIR_ID").size().values)
summary = SKY[["PAIR_ID", "skycell_name", "zodi_bin", "coverage_bin",
                "count_0p25"]].merge(l2_counts, on="PAIR_ID", how="left")
summary["n_l2_files"] = summary["n_l2_files"].fillna(0).astype(int)
summary["n_visits"] = summary["n_visits"].fillna(0).astype(int)
summary
```
