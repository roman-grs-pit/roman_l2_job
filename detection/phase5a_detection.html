Phase 5a — source detection + efficiency baseline

Default SourceCatalogStep run on pair 11119’s mosaic; star/galaxy completeness curves

What Phase 5a does

Phase 5a runs source detection on the Phase-4a mosaic and measures per-magnitude detection efficiency vs the Phase-3 truth catalog. It establishes the baseline (default SourceCatalogStep parameters) before the kernel_fwhm sweep of Phase 5b.

Pipeline:

  1. Read the MosaicPipeline side-effect catalog (*_cat.parquet) — equivalent to running SourceCatalogStep standalone with default parameters (kernel_fwhm=2.0, snr_threshold=3.0, npixels=25, deblend=False).
  2. Crossmatch stars truth and galaxies truth separately against the recovered catalog via astropy.match_coordinates_sky, cross-match radius 0.3″.
  3. Binned efficiency = fraction of truth sources in each magnitude bin with a recovered match inside the radius.
  4. Count “false positives” = recovered sources with no truth within the crossmatch radius.

quantity stars (PSF) galaxies (SER n=1, r=0.275″)
90% completeness magnitude 25.25 24.53
50% completeness magnitude 25.40 24.65

The galaxy curve turns over ~0.75 mag shallower than the star curve — expected because a Sersic profile spreads the flux of a fixed total magnitude over several arcsec², lowering the peak SNR in the detection kernel. The default kernel_fwhm=2.0 pix (≈0.11″) is matched to the F158 PSF, which under-weights galaxies; Phase 5b sweeps the kernel to explicitly study this.

False positives: 0/282 within 0.3″. Every recovered source sits inside the crossmatch radius of at least one truth source. This is partly by design (truth covers the skycell densely) and partly a quality check on the pipeline — no spurious detections.

Why the galaxies hit an earlier wall

  • The F158 PSF FWHM is ~0.11″ at the detector plane; a circular exponential galaxy with r_eff = 0.275″ extends to ≥2× that.
  • SourceCatalogStep convolves the image with a Gaussian of kernel_fwhm = 2.0 pix (≈0.11″) and applies snr_threshold = 3 to the convolved image. The convolution essentially integrates flux under a PSF-matched kernel. For point sources this kernel is matched-filter-optimal; for resolved sources it loses up to ~50% of the signal because flux outside the PSF radius is not weighted.
  • Raising kernel_fwhm to ~5-7 pix would better match the galaxy surface-brightness profile and push the galaxy completeness curve ~0.3-0.5 mag deeper, at the cost of slightly higher false-positive rates and blending.

Phase 5b will quantify that tradeoff.

Comparison to the full run’s depth

The full-run mosaic (configs/full.yaml, 6 pair-only visits at target RA 10°, Dec 0°) measured Kron-based 50% completeness at F158 mag 23.95 across all depth levels, and 24.05 at the design depth ≥ 6 — shallower than our 24.65 (galaxies) / 25.40 (stars) here. The gap comes from two effects:

  1. Depth: our pair 11119 mosaic reaches per-pixel depth ~10 (after the Phase 2 HLWAS expansion), the full-run design-depth skycells only reach depth 6. Moving from depth 6 to 10 is sqrt(10/6) = 1.29× better SNR, ≈ 0.28 mag depth gain.
  2. Detection method: the full-run’s mag_50pct uses a Kron-like aperture metric based on detected flux; we use a position-based “was a source found” criterion. The latter is more forgiving for faint extended sources.

Both contribute plausibly to our ~0.5-1 mag deeper result.

Checkpoint

Phase 5a criteria met: pipeline works end-to-end, clean efficiency curves, physically-sensible star-vs-galaxy offset, zero false positives at default parameters. Ready for either:

  • Phase 4b — scale to all 5 (or 25) skycells (after the proposed skycell-driven refactor of Phases 1-2).
  • Phase 5b — kernel_fwhm ∈ {2, 3, 5, 7} sweep on the current mosaic, cheaper (just reruns SourceCatalogStep).
