Phase 1 — skycell selection

12 target skycells in a (zodi × coverage) matrix across the HLWAS F158 footprint

What Phase 1 does

Pick N target skycells inside the HLWAS F158 Wide-Field2 footprint, stratified by:

  • Ecliptic latitude (proxy for zodiacal background). Bands: |lat| ≤ 10° (high zodi), 15°–30° (mid), ≥ 40° (low). Gaps between bands ensure cross-bin picks aren’t at adjacent |ecl_lat|.
  • Local HLWAS coverage density (proxy for mosaic depth): count_0p25 = # HLWAS F158 exposures whose centre lies within 0.25° of the skycell centre. “High” = ≥ 75th percentile; “low” = ≤ 25th percentile, across HLWAS-interior candidates.

The candidate pool is all skymap skycells in the HLWAS Dec band for which count_0p6 (# F158 exposures within 0.6°) is ≥ 5. That filter is meant to exclude the outer survey edges where only isolated pointings sit nearby. It’s lenient, though: a skycell sitting in a gap between HLWAS visit tiles can still have 5+ exposures at the 0.3-0.6° annulus while having zero within 0.25°. In the current run, pair 6 (RA 26.7°, Dec −21.3°) is exactly such a case — it looks interior on this coarse filter but sits in a real tiling gap, and Phase 2 expansion ultimately finds only 2 L2 files overlapping its selected skycell. The “coverage gap” category we produce is therefore a mix of true survey-edge skycells (pairs 2, 11) and interior-but-gap skycells (pairs 1, 5, 6) — the mosaics show both kinds hit depth 0 for the median interior pixel.

Target: 2 picks per cell × 3 zodi bands × 2 coverage bands = 12 skycells. A 5° great-circle minimum separation is enforced between any two picks so different cells don’t accidentally land on top of each other.

Per skycell, we also compute the sim date that minimises solar elongation inside [90°, 115°] in 2026 — the “hardest-zodi” day within Roman’s pointing constraint for that field.

Caveats from the first run

Some “low coverage” cells produce no mosaic. After Phase 2 expansion, the high_zodi × low_coverage cell (pairs 9 and 10) ends up with zero L2 files overlapping the selected skycell — HLWAS’s tiling near the ecliptic is sparse enough that even outer SCAs don’t reach “gap” skycells there. We proceed with the other 10 skycells for Phase 4b; the missing cell is documented but not recovered in this pass. A future iteration could tighten the “low coverage” definition to count_0p25 ∈ [1, 4] instead of ≤ 0 to guarantee non-zero overlap.

The interior filter is too lenient. count_0p6 ≥ 5 accepts both true survey-interior skycells and “tiling-gap” skycells where the 0.25° neighbourhood is empty but the 0.3-0.6° annulus isn’t. Tightening to e.g. count_0p25 ≥ 1 AND count_0p6 ≥ 10 would force the candidate pool to skycells that at least one HLWAS pointing’s inner SCAs can reach — eliminating the depth-0 mosaic outcomes by construction rather than after-the-fact.

## Caveats from the first run

**Some "low coverage" cells produce no mosaic.** After Phase 2
expansion, the `high_zodi × low_coverage` cell (pairs 9 and 10) ends
up with **zero** L2 files overlapping the selected skycell — HLWAS's
tiling near the ecliptic is sparse enough that even outer SCAs
don't reach "gap" skycells there. We proceed with the other 10
skycells for Phase 4b; the missing cell is documented but not
recovered in this pass. A future iteration could tighten the "low
coverage" definition to `count_0p25 ∈ [1, 4]` instead of `≤ 0` to
guarantee non-zero overlap.

**The interior filter is too lenient.** `count_0p6 ≥ 5` accepts both
true survey-interior skycells and "tiling-gap" skycells where the
0.25° neighbourhood is empty but the 0.3-0.6° annulus isn't.

the candidate pool to skycells that at least one HLWAS pointing's
inner SCAs can reach — eliminating the depth-0 mosaic outcomes by
