Phase 6 — cross-mosaic summary

Detection efficiency vs zodi × coverage × parameter choice across 10 skycells

What Phase 6 does

Roll up per-mosaic Phase 5 results from the skycell-driven Phase 4b run (10 mosaics, 12 originally selected skycells less 2 in an HLWAS coverage gap). For each mosaic we have:

  • Mosaic median interior depth (from the coadd context array).
  • Baseline detection efficiency (default SourceCatalogStep params).
  • Kernel sweep (kernel_fwhm 2 / 3 / 5 / 7) and npixels sweep (npixels 9 / 16 / 25).

Plus Phase-1 metadata: ecliptic latitude, zodi band, romanisim sky prediction.

Takeaways

  1. npixels=16 is a universal win (+0.5 mag star completeness, +0.1 mag galaxy) at every depth and zodi band tested. The per-mosaic cost is zero — it just re-runs SourceCatalogStep.
  2. Depth drives completeness much more than zodi. In the depth ≥6 regime, zodi variation contributes ~0.1-0.3 mag; the depth spread from 0 → 10 contributes ~2 mag.
  3. The kernel_fwhm=2 default is correct across the board. Wider kernels hurt at high depth (blending), break even at low depth.
  4. Depth-0 (“coverage gap”) skycells give very noisy curves — not useful for characterising the HLWAS-nominal detection regime. Worth redesigning Phase 1 to pick “low coverage” at 1-3 exposures within 0.25° (rather than ≤0) so the low-depth tier still has partial mosaic coverage.

Checkpoint

Phase 6 deliverable. The skycell-driven design works end-to-end at 12-skycell scale. Parameter recommendations from pair 11119 (Phase 5c) generalise to the full sample.

   ≥6 regime, zodi variation contributes ~0.1-0.3 mag; the depth
   spread from 0 → 10 contributes ~2 mag.

   kernels hurt at high depth (blending), break even at low depth.
4. **Depth-0 ("coverage gap") skycells** give very noisy curves — not
   useful for characterising the HLWAS-nominal detection regime.
   Worth redesigning Phase 1 to pick "low coverage" at 1-3 exposures
   within 0.25° (rather than ≤0) so the low-depth tier still has
